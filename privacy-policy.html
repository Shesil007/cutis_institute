Privacy Policy

Cutis Institute of Dermatology & Aesthetic Sciences

At Cutis Institute of Dermatology & Aesthetic Sciences, your privacy is of utmost importance to us. As a leading skin clinic in Calicut specializing in dermatology, cosmetology, and plastic surgery, we are committed to safeguarding the personal information you share with us during your journey towards healthier and more radiant skin.

Effective Date: April 8, 2026

1. Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of information from our patients and website visitors:

  • Personal Identification Information: Name, email address, phone number, address, age, and gender.
  • Medical Information: Health history, treatment records, prescriptions, and photographs (where applicable for diagnostic or documentation purposes).
  • Payment Details: Billing and transaction information (processed securely via third-party payment gateways).
  • Website Usage Data: IP address, browser type, device information, and browsing patterns on our website.

2. How We Use Your Information

The information we collect is used to:

  • Provide accurate diagnosis, treatment, and follow-up care.
  • Schedule appointments and manage patient records.
  • Respond to inquiries or provide requested services.
  • Improve our website, services, and patient experience.
  • Send appointment reminders, health tips, and promotional updates (with your consent).
  • Comply with legal and regulatory requirements.

3. Data Protection and Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your personal and medical information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Access to your information is restricted to authorized staff who are trained in handling sensitive health data with confidentiality.

4. Sharing Your Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share your data only when:

  • Required by law or legal proceedings.
  • Referred to or coordinated with another healthcare provider (with your consent).
  • Used by third-party service providers who assist us in delivering services (such as laboratory services, payment processors, or IT providers), under strict confidentiality agreements.

5. Patient Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access your medical records and personal data.
  • Request corrections to inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Withdraw consent for certain uses of your information.
  • Request that we delete your data (where legally permissible).

6. Website and Cookies

Our website may use cookies to enhance user experience and collect anonymized analytical data. You can choose to disable cookies through your browser settings, although this may affect website functionality.

7. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites. Please note that we are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites. We encourage you to review their privacy policies before submitting any personal information.

8. Children's Privacy

Our services are intended for individuals aged 18 and above. We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors without verified parental or guardian consent.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or legal obligations. Updates will be posted on this page with a revised effective date.
